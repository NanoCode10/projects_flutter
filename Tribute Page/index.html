Diego A. Maradona

Diego Armando Maradona 1 ( Lanús , October 30, 1960 - Dique Luján , November 25, 2020 ) 8 was an Argentine soccer player and coach . As a player, he worked as an attacking midfielder or striker , and is recognized by numerous specialists , 9 ​10 former soccer players and international personalities 11​ as " one of the best soccer players in history ." 12Likewise, he has been listed by some media as the "best player in the history of the World Cup ", of which he was designated as the best player in its 1986 edition . 13 In the FIFA Player of the Century awards , he was selected as the " best footballer of the 20th century ". In the popular vote, he obtained the third position in the vote of the experts selected by FIFA, n. 1 and achieved fifth place in the vote carried out by the IFFHS . 14 In the 2012 Globe Soccer Awards edition, he was distinguished as the best « Player of the 20th Century». 15 16 For his legendary figure in sports, which earned him the nickname Pibe de Oro , as well as for his extravagant, temperamental and charismatic personality, and for his troubled life outside of football, where he was suspended for doping in 1991 and 1994, Maradona is considered one of the most historical figures of the Argentine Republic, and one of its greatest representatives in the rest of the world. 17 ​18 ​19 ​20​ Likewise, his person has been the subject of the most varied references in Argentine and Neapolitan popular culture. 21 ​22 Raised in the Villa Fiorito neighborhood , Maradona was signed to the youth divisions of Argentinos Juniors , where he spent five seasons, earning the record of being the top scorer in the Argentine championship five consecutive times. In 1981, he was transferred to Boca Juniors , 23 where he obtained the Metropolitan Championship , his only title in Argentina. After the 1982 World Cup in Spain , Maradona became the first footballer to achieve the record for being the most expensive transfer in the world twice, by being transferred to Barcelona for €7.30 million, and then to Napoli in Italy for 12 million euros. 24In Spain, Diego would obtain three national titles before ending up in Italy in 1984. There, Maradona became one of the most important public figures in Naples , 25 by leading the team to win the scudetto twice (1987, 1990 ) and the UEFA Cup , the institution's only international title. After seven seasons as a Neapolitan, in which he finished as the all-time top scorer, Maradona left Italy after obtaining his first positive for doping in the 1990-91 season. In the final stage of his career, he played for Sevilla and Newell's Old Boys before returning to Boca Juniors in 1995 and finally retiring in 1997.26 With the Argentine National Team , Maradona was champion of the 1979 Youth World Cup , and with the seniors of the 1986 World Cup in Mexico as team captain, in which he would star in one of the most outstanding individual performances in the history of the sport , 2728 29 by scoring the two famous goals that gave victory to his team in the match against England in the quarterfinals, the first of them known as " the hand of God " and the second as the " Goal of the Century ", designated by a FIFA vote as the best in the history of the 20th century World Cups .30 In Italy 1990 , Argentina would almost repeat the same feat, but would end up as runner-up. After three years of absence due to his doping problems, Maradona returned to help Argentina qualify for the 1994 World Cup in the United States , a tournament in which Diego would again test positive for drugs when finding ephedrine in his samples, being expelled. of the competition, which contributed to the subsequent elimination of Argentina in the round of 16. 31 This would be his last participation at the national team level as a player. After his retirement as a footballer, his impact as a player inspired the Maradonian Church , he was a television host in both Italy and Argentina, 32 and vice president of the Boca Juniors Football Commission between 2005 and 2006, before launching his career as a director technical. After short experiences in the 1990s, in October 2008 Maradona was appointed coach of the Argentine National Team for the 2010 World Cup in South Africa . After an agonizing classification in the Qualifiers, in the tournament Argentina would be eliminated in the quarterfinals against Germany. After his time in the National Team, he coached the Arab clubs Al-Wasl and Al-Fujairah, to the second division Mexican club Dorados de Sinaloa , and from 2019 until his death in 2020 to Gimnasia y Esgrima La Plata , of the Argentine First Division . In addition, he was honorary president of Dinamo Brest between July and September 2018.
